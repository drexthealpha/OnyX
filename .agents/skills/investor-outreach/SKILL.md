# Investor Outreach

Professional, concise outreach that respects investors' time and gets to the point.

## When to Activate

- Sending cold outreach emails
- Managing warm introductions
- Writing follow-up sequences
- Creating investor updates
- Building investor relationships

## Cold Email Template

```
Subject: [One specific thing about them] + [Your one-liner]

[Personalization line — something specific about them or their portfolio]

[2-3 sentences]
- What you do (one sentence)
- Why it matters (one sentence)
- One specific metric or signal

[Call to action — keep it simple]
```

## Follow-Up Sequence

### Day 1: Initial Email
Send the main email above.

### Day 4: Follow-Up 1
```
Subject: Following up on [original subject]

[Short — reference the original]

Curious if this missed your inbox. Happy to share more details on [specific aspect].
```

### Day 10: Follow-Up 2
```
Subject: Quick update on [company]

[One update — creates urgency/logic]

Would still love to chat if there's timing.
```

### Day 21: Final
```
Subject: Last one — [update or milestone]

[One final signal or milestone]

Best of luck with [something about them]. I'll stop here.
```

## Warm Introduction

```
Hi [Name],

[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out. They're excited about what we're building.

Quick summary:
- [One line on what you do]
- [One line on traction or milestone]
- [One line on why reaching out]

Happy to work around your calendar.

[Your name]
```

## Investor Updates

```
Subject: [Company] — [One highlight or number]

Updates:
- [Metric] now [value] (+[growth] MoM)
- [Milestone] completed
- [Team/news] announcement

[URL for more]

Thanks,
[Name]
```

## Key Principles

- Keep emails under 150 words
- One ask per email
- Personalize with something specific
- Always include a signal or milestone
- Make it easy to say yes or no

## What Works

- Specific personalization
- Clear signal or metric
- Low-friction ask
- Time-bound context
